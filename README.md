# Heart Disease Analysis (EDA)

## Project Overview

This project performs Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) on a Heart Disease dataset to identify the major risk factors associated with heart disease and understand patient health patterns. The objective is to generate insights that can help healthcare providers improve preventive care and early diagnosis.

---

## Problem Statement

HealthPulse Analytics aims to identify key factors contributing to heart disease and develop a profile of high-risk patients. By analyzing patient demographics, clinical measurements, and medical history, the project seeks to support better healthcare decision-making and preventive interventions.

---

## Objectives

* Analyze patient demographics and clinical measurements.
* Identify major risk factors associated with heart disease.
* Compare characteristics of patients with and without heart disease.
* Study relationships among important health indicators.
* Generate actionable healthcare recommendations.

---

## Dataset Information

**Dataset Source:**

Heart Disease Dataset

The dataset contains information such as:

* Age
* Gender
* Chest Pain Type
* Resting Blood Pressure
* Cholesterol Level
* Fasting Blood Sugar
* ECG Results
* Maximum Heart Rate
* Exercise-Induced Angina
* ST Depression (Oldpeak)
* Number of Major Vessels
* Thalassemia Type
* Presence of Heart Disease (Target)

## Key Findings

* Certain chest pain types are strongly associated with heart disease.
* Exercise-induced angina is more common among high-risk patients.
* Patients with a higher number of major vessels tend to show greater disease prevalence.
* Maximum heart rate and ST depression provide important diagnostic indicators.
* Multiple clinical factors collectively contribute to heart disease risk.

---

## Business Recommendations

* Promote regular cardiovascular screening programs.
* Focus preventive healthcare initiatives on high-risk patient groups.
* Encourage lifestyle modifications for patients with elevated cholesterol and blood pressure.
* Improve monitoring of patients showing multiple risk factors.
* Support early diagnosis through data-driven risk assessment models.

---

## Conclusion

The analysis highlights several important factors associated with heart disease risk. Understanding these relationships can support healthcare professionals in early detection, preventive interventions, and personalized treatment planning, ultimately improving patient outcomes.
